Dissertation User Trial

This is the training stage of the trial. In this page you are met with 36 squares in a 6 by 6 grid. The grid is labelled in the same way as a spreadsheet or battleships board, with the rows being labelled A through F and Columns 1 to 6. The purpose of this test is to get used to the shape of the grid and how it is possible to move around within the grid.

Once you press the start button, you will be able to use the A, S, W and D keys to move around the grid.

These keys are used in place of the directional inputs, with W representing up, D representing right, S representing Down and A representing left.

You will begin in the grid square A1 and your screen reader should indicate your grid position after each movement.

If you wish to turn off the screen reader updates, press the M key to toggle Mute.

By default the screen will show a graphic of your movement, to remove this graphic press the N key to toggle the graphic.

Please take your time to grasp the concept and how your entries move you through the grid as this will make the process easier. To reset your position in the grid, press the R key.

Note: The grid has no real edges and allows you to wrap around. For example you can move from the leftmost column to the rightmost by moving left in that column, you could travel from A4 to F4 with the A key. You may also return to a grid square you have already used. These are to ensure users can have access to the most possible movements at all times.
